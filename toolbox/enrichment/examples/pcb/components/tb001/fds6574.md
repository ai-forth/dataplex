## Summary

`FDS6574` is one of the highest-value component records to normalize for TB001
because it appears in both the page-4 and page-5 control/power boundary and is
currently the largest blocker to stronger simulation confidence.

## Known From Current Board Evidence

* The part is identified as `FDS6574`.
* The package currently associated with it is `SOIC-8`.
* It appears as `Q1` on page 4.
* It appears as `Q1`, `Q5`, `Q7`, `Q8`, `Q10`, and `Q11` on page 5.
* Current corpus interpretation treats it as part of the higher-risk switching
  family rather than a passive or low-consequence support part.

## Manufacturer Characteristic Status

Current status is no longer just a stub, but it is still incomplete.

Known:

* MPN string: `FDS6574A`
* manufacturer: onsemi / ON Semiconductor
* package evidence in the current TB001 corpus: `SOIC-8`
* manufacturer datasheet polarity: N-channel
* manufacturer datasheet `VDSS`: `20 V`
* manufacturer datasheet `VGSS`: `±8 V`
* manufacturer datasheet continuous `ID`: `16 A`
* manufacturer datasheet pulsed `ID`: `80 A`
* manufacturer datasheet `RDS(on) max`:
  * `6 mΩ` at `VGS = 4.5 V`
  * `7 mΩ` at `VGS = 2.5 V`
  * `9 mΩ` at `VGS = 1.8 V`
* manufacturer datasheet thermal characteristics:
  * `RθJA = 50 °C/W`
  * `RθJC = 25 °C/W`
* manufacturer datasheet operating/storage junction range: `-55 °C to +175 °C`

Still required from manufacturer sources:

* verified pinout
* extraction of the most relevant capacitance and gate-charge values
* local capture of a vendor SPICE model or justified surrogate

## Manufacturer Source Intake

Current primary-source status:

* TB001 now includes a local manufacturer datasheet copy at
  `../TB001/datasheets/FDS6574A-D.PDF`.
* That datasheet identifies `FDS6574A` as an onsemi `N-channel` PowerTrench
  MOSFET intended for `DC/DC converter` use.
* This resolves the highest-risk prior uncertainty: the device family should
  not be treated as an unresolved PMOS-class part in the
  manufacturer-grounded layer.

Current practical rule:

* use the BOM-backed `SOIC-8` package fact
* use the local onsemi datasheet to anchor polarity, voltage, current, and
  on-resistance characteristics
* treat any PMOS-style simulation of `FDS6574` as a retired comparison
  artifact until a board-level topology note explains why that behavior would
  still be needed

## Characteristic Catalog Status

At this stage, `FDS6574A` is no longer a pure catalog stub, but it is not yet a
fully normalized characteristic record either.

That distinction matters. We now have enough evidence to reject the earlier
placeholder polarity assumption while still being honest about what the board
topology does and does not prove.

## Simulation Relevance

This part still dominates simulation uncertainty in the page-5 switching cases.

The key open question is no longer the device polarity. It is now how an
onsemi N-channel power MOSFET is actually being used in the TB001 page-5
switching structure.

That means the next simulation step should move away from PMOS high-side
abstractions and toward topology hypotheses that are compatible with a real
N-channel device.

## Next Catalog Step

Normalize the pinout and dynamic characteristics from the local onsemi
datasheet, then replace the retired PMOS placeholder with an N-channel-aligned
simulation model and topology note for the TB001 cases.
